Ludwig Van Beethoven

The Titan Of Classical Music

Here's a timeline of events in Beethovens life:

• 1770:

Born On December 16, 1770, in Bonn, Germany.

• 1787:

Beethoven makes his first public performance as a pianist.

• 1792:

He moves to Vienna, Austria, where he would spend much of his life and career.

• 1795:

Beethoven gives his first public concert in Vienna as a pianist and composer.

• 1796:

Bethoven begins to experience hearing loss, a condition that would progressively worsen throughout his life.

• 1801:

He writes his famous "Heiligenstadt Testament", a letter to his brothers in which he reveals his despair over his hearing loss but resolves to continue his musical pursuits.

• 1804 ‐ 1808:

Beethoven completes his Third Symphony, also known as the "Erioca", dedicated to Napolean Bonaparte ( although he later removes he dedication due to political reasons).

• 1814 ‐ 1815:

He premieres his Symphony No. 7 and Symphony No. 8

• 1816 ‐ 1819:

Beethoven composes his famous "Piano Sonata No. 29 in B-flat Major," also known as the "Hammerklavier Sonata," one of his most technically challenging works.

• 1817:

He composes his "Missa Solemnis," a grand and monumental choral work.

• 1824:

Beethoven premieres his Ninth Symphony, which includes the "Ode to Joy." This symphony is notable for its use of a choir in the final movement, a groundbreaking innovation in symphonic music.

• 1827:

Ludwig Van Beethoven passes away on March 26, 1827, in Vienna, Austria, at the age of 56.

"Beethoven, who was much before his time in thought and feeling, is most conspicuous in his works by the triumph of mind over matter, the victory of the intellectual over the physical powers. He would never have written an opera; he would have scorned it. The contrast of Beethoven's orchestration is the finest and subtlest in the world."

‐‐ Richard Wagner, Renowned Composer and Conductor
